Ericsson and Semcon

The project concerned how to improve the remote collaboration context and the experience of a remote meeting at Ericsson. The aim was to aid users to collaborate more, as the climate in this large company environment combined with technical equipment and available digital tools were hindering the more creative and spontanious environments on remote. The dynamics between participants, some being more dominant and some being more passive, were found to widely affect the outcome and quality of the meeting. We questioned oureselves: how can a user feel inclued and "there" when being thousands of miles away from the person(s) they are having a conversation with? How could we include the users more in the digital meeting room?

The project resulted in a software prototype which aims to adress these topics and provide a solution. It aids the users through encouraging more individual participation: both in the physical meeting room evironment and on a distance. This is achieved through integrating interactive components to increase expressability, such as opinionated buttons and icons to be able to call for attention. Furthermore did the software transcribe speech to text in order to remove the need of a secretary. A role which often is separated from the collaborative space. The software also simplified how to start a meeting as it is a browserbased direct link to the meeting room rather than a program that needs to be installed.

Contextual inqury, interviews, observational studies and mock-up user testing were a few of the methods used. Adobe Creative suite (Photoshop, Illustrator, Indesign) were used for the concepting and presentation material. The project was carried through from September 2015 to February 2016.

The final product is aimed as a simple tool for remote collaboration - supporting creative sessions, presentations and fluid communication.
